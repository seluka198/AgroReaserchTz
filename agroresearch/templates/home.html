AGRO-Research TZ
    Agriculture is a key pillar of Tanzania’s economy, employing over 65% of the population and driving food security, exports, and rural development. In the Southern Highlands—Rukwa, Katavi, Mbeya, and Songwe the sector is central to livelihoods, with major crops like maize, rice, beans, and coffee. Mbeya leads in coffee and horticulture, while Rukwa and Katavi have abundant but underutilized land. Songwe holds trade potential but faces challenges in irrigation and input access. Common issues across the regions include poor infrastructure, limited use of technology, and weak extension services. However, there is strong potential for growth through climate-smart practices, digital innovation, and targeted investments.
