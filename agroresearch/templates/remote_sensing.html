Remote Sensing

Remote Sensing is the process of acquiring information about the Earth's surface without direct physical contact. It involves using satellites, aircraft, drones, and other remote technologies to collect data.

Applications of Remote Sensing

  • Agriculture: Monitoring crop health, soil moisture, and predicting yields.
  • Environmental Monitoring: Detecting deforestation, climate change, and pollution.
  • Disaster Management: Assessing flood impact, earthquake damage, and forest fires.
  • Urban Planning: Mapping land use, infrastructure development, and city growth.
  • Hydrology: Monitoring water bodies, groundwater levels, and precipitation patterns.

Types of Remote Sensing

  • Passive Remote Sensing: Uses natural sunlight to capture images (e.g., optical satellites like Landsat).
  • Active Remote Sensing: Uses sensors that emit signals (e.g., RADAR, LiDAR).

Popular Remote Sensing Satellites

  • Landsat Series
  • Sentinel-2
  • MODIS
  • ASTER
  • RADARSAT

Conclusion

Remote sensing plays a vital role in various fields, providing valuable data for decision-making, research, and sustainable development.
